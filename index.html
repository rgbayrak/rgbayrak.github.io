PhD THESIS

Computational Methods to Advance Individual Precision in Brain Mapping

My research centers around one of the key gaps in our understanding of the mechanisms of brain function, inter-individual variability. Interpretation of large-scale and high dimensional neuroimaging data at the level of individuals is crucial for characterizing brain function in health and disease, as well as for understanding what makes us unique. The overarching goal of my thesis is to find generalizable mappings between observed data and biomarkers of human cognition and behavior, by leveraging deep learning methods.
